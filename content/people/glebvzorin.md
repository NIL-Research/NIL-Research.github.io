Dr. Gleb Vzorin is an ongoing postdoctoral researcher at the Neurocognitive Image Laboratory,
Shanghai International Studies University (SISU), and a research scientist at the Institute of
Psychology, Russian Academy of Sciences. He earned his PhD at the Russian Academy of
Sciences under the supervision of Prof. D. Ushakov, focusing on the impact of digital
technologies on human memory. His earlier research at Lomonosov Moscow State
University, conducted under the guidance of Prof. V. Nourkova and Prof. K. Anokhin,
explored memory consolidation mechanisms under the influence of pharmacological
interventions. Dr. Vzorin is the author of more than 17 peer-reviewed articles, one registered
patent, and over 30 scientific conference presentations. At Lomonosov Moscow State
University, he lectured and tutored courses in Experimental Psychology, Psychology of
Intelligence, Mathematical Methods and Digital Technologies in Psychology, and Psychology
of Motivation and Emotion. He also served as Coordinator of the academic program Big
Data, Artificial Intelligence, and Cognitive Systems and chaired the Scientific Student Society.
His current research interests span human–machine co-evolution, the methodology of
cognitive science, and neuroaesthetics.
