## Overview

NIL Conference 2025, **"Image Neuroscience and the Arts,"** was held from **October 19 to 25, 2025** in Shanghai, China. The conference brought together researchers, artists, theorists, and scholars from multiple disciplines to explore the relationship between image, brain, aesthetic experience, creative expression, and the arts.

The conference focused on **Image Neuroscience** as an emerging interdisciplinary field concerned with the neural basis of aesthetic experience, visual perception, artistic creation, and image-based cognition. It provided an academic platform for examining how the brain responds to artistic and visual stimuli, how aesthetic judgment may be understood through cognitive and neural mechanisms, and how the arts can contribute to new conceptual frameworks for understanding the human mind.

Under the theme **"Image Neuroscience and the Arts,"** the conference connected cognitive neuroscience, neuroaesthetics, psychology, image studies, art history, digital media, philosophy, linguistics, and artistic practice. Through keynote lectures, conference sessions, open discussions, cultural visits, and institutional exchange, the event fostered interdisciplinary dialogue and international collaboration around image-based research and the scientific study of art.

The conference was also associated with the **23rd Conference on International Exchange of Professionals**, during which the inauguration ceremony of the Neurocognitive Image Lab (NIL) and related institutional exchange activities took place.

## Organizers

The conference was hosted by **Shanghai International Studies University (SISU)** and organized by:

- Neurocognitive Image Lab (NIL), SISU
- World Arthistory Institute (WAI), SISU
- Institute of Language Sciences (ILS), SISU
- Office of International Cooperation and Exchange (OICE), SISU

The conference was held with support from national and municipal partners associated with the 23rd Conference on International Exchange of Professionals.

## Program Snapshot

The conference program included several connected components across Shanghai and SISU:

- Arrival, registration, and reception activities
- Cultural visits and academic exchange in Shanghai
- Participation in the 23rd Conference on International Exchange of Professionals
- Inauguration ceremony of the Neurocognitive Image Lab (NIL)
- Institutional exchange and signing of memoranda of understanding
- Multi-session academic conference on image neuroscience and the arts
- Conference sessions at the Expo Center and at SISU
- Open discussions, comments, and concluding reflections
- Continuation into the Autumn School program after the conference

The SISU sessions included presentations and discussions on topics such as creative inference, temporal windows in the brain, eye movements in aesthetic perception, photography and neuroimaging, image and word interaction, art perception, neural self-processing, fractal contours, and the neurobiology of aesthetics.

## Academic Themes

The conference addressed questions including:

- How aesthetic experience is represented and processed in the brain
- How artistic creation and visual perception can be studied through cognitive neuroscience
- How image-based research can connect neuroscience, art history, psychology, linguistics, and philosophy
- How digital media, artistic practice, and neuroimaging may jointly contribute to new understandings of cognition
- How cross-cultural and interdisciplinary dialogue can shape the future of image neuroscience
